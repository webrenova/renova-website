PPM Benefits

What are the benefits of
implementing a PPM schedule?

Seven practical reasons why planned preventative maintenance pays for itself — and keeps your building running smoothly year after year.

A PPM (Planned Preventative Maintenance) schedule keeps a building in good working order through regular, planned checks and servicing. It helps cut the risk of costly breakdowns, reduces downtime, keeps you compliant with regulations, and extends the lifespan of your kit — all while making life more comfortable for the people using the space.

And let's be honest, unexpected failures are a nightmare for any facilities manager or building owner. A good PPM schedule takes a proactive approach, catching little issues before they grow into big, expensive repairs. In facilities management, that's one of the surest ways to keep a building running well year after year.

1. Lower Reactive Maintenance Costs

One of the biggest benefits of PPM is how it cuts reactive maintenance costs. Emergency repairs nearly always cost more — there's the call-out fee, the rush on parts, and sometimes extra damage caused by the breakdown itself. A planned maintenance schedule can reduce maintenance costs by around 12–18% over time. Think about spotting a small leak during an inspection instead of waiting for it to soak a ceiling and cause thousands in damage. A structured PPM programme keeps spending predictable and helps avoid those nasty budget shocks. Any FM will tell you it's far better to schedule maintenance than to gamble on nothing going wrong.

2. Better Energy Efficiency

PPM ensures building services such as HVAC systems, boilers, and lighting controls are kept in good working order. Regular inspections and tune-ups mean equipment runs as it should, which can knock 10–15% off your energy bills. Something as simple as cleaning filters or checking that heating and cooling systems aren't "fighting" each other can save a surprising amount. Over time, those savings add up — and they can even bump up your Energy Performance Certificate (EPC) rating, which is handy for compliance and sustainability.

3. Longer Asset Lifespan

Following manufacturer guidelines and recognised standards like SFG20 keeps your building's big-ticket assets working for longer. HVAC, lifts, electrical systems — they'll all give you more years if you stay on top of maintenance. I've seen a £200 part swap during a routine check save a client from a £5,000 repair. This approach to maintenance means you get the most out of your kit and keep warranties valid too.

4. Stay Compliant with Regulations

UK building owners have a fair few legal obligations to meet under health and safety, fire safety, and energy efficiency rules. PPM tasks like emergency lighting tests, boiler servicing, and electrical inspections all help keep the building compliant. An up-to-date maintenance calendar and logbook make life much easier when you're audited — and it's far less stressful than dealing with fines or enforcement notices after the fact.

5. Improved Tenant Satisfaction

Happy tenants tend to stick around. That's why a facilities manager will always value smooth-running building services. A broken lift or a faulty HVAC system can sour the mood quickly. Planned maintenance keeps systems running smoothly and helps minimise disruption to tenants' daily operations. A responsive, proactive approach to repairs builds trust and can be a deciding factor when tenants consider renewing leases.

6. Reduced Risk and Downtime

A PPM plan is one of the best ways to protect against unexpected failures that can grind a business to a halt. Unplanned downtime from equipment failure can cost UK businesses billions every year. Regular maintenance inspections pick up early warning signs, reduce the risk of accidents, and keep the place safe for staff and visitors. Fixing things before they fail is almost always more cost-effective than scrambling after a breakdown.

7. Supports Sustainability Goals

Preventive maintenance isn't just about money — it's about reducing waste and energy use too. Optimising equipment performance and getting a longer lifespan out of it means fewer replacements and less scrap heading to landfill. It also keeps energy use down, which is good for your bills and your ESG targets. Even small fixes — a sealed air leak, a rebalanced HVAC system — make a difference to both your carbon footprint and the comfort of the people inside.

A well-thought-out PPM strategy is one of the most cost-effective maintenance strategies a facilities manager can put in place. Whether you use a CAFM system to track maintenance tasks or just keep a clear maintenance calendar, the aim's the same — prevent problems before they disrupt your building. PPM protects your building, your budget, and your reputation — and keeps your buildings running smoothly in the long term.
