Legal Requirement

Fire door inspection is
a legal obligation

Under the Regulatory Reform (Fire Safety) Order 2005, the Responsible Person in any commercial premises must ensure fire doors are maintained in efficient working order. The Fire Safety Act 2021 and Fire Safety (England) Regulations 2022 extended these requirements to residential buildings with two or more storeys.

Failure to maintain fire doors can lead to enforcement action, prosecution, and — most critically — puts building occupants at serious risk. A professional fire door inspection provides the documented evidence that your building is compliant and your fire safety obligations are being met.

What We Check

What a Fire Door Inspection Covers

Get a quote →

Our fire door inspection is carried out to BS 9999 and BS 8214 standards and covers every critical component of the door assembly. Every inspection is documented with photographs and a written compliance report.

🚪
Door Leaf & Frame Condition

Visual inspection of the door leaf and frame for damage, warping, delamination or unauthorised modifications that could compromise fire resistance.

📏
Gap Tolerance Checks

Measurement of all gaps between door leaf, frame and floor. Sides and top must not exceed 3mm; the gap at the floor must not exceed 8mm unless a threshold seal is fitted.

🔥
Intumescent Strips & Smoke Seals

Inspection of intumescent strips and cold smoke seals for correct fitting, damage, absence or incorrect specification — these are critical to the door's fire rating.

🔄
Self-Closing Devices

Testing of door closers to confirm the door closes fully and latches from any open position under its own power — a mandatory requirement for fire doors.

🔩
Hinges & Ironmongery

Inspection of hinge type, number, fixing and condition. Checks that all ironmongery — handles, locks, bolts, holds-open devices — is fire-rated and correctly fitted.

🪟
Glazing & Signage

Confirmation that any vision panels use correctly rated fire glass, that beading is fire-rated, and that mandatory 'Fire Door Keep Shut' or 'Fire Door Keep Locked' signage is in place.

Frequently Asked Questions

How often should fire doors be inspected? ▼
The Regulatory Reform (Fire Safety) Order 2005 requires the Responsible Person to maintain fire doors in efficient working order. As a general rule, fire doors in high-traffic areas (stairwells, corridors) should be inspected every 6 months, with lower-traffic areas inspected annually. Your fire risk assessment will define the required frequency for your building.
Who can carry out a fire door inspection? ▼
Fire door inspections should be carried out by a competent person with appropriate training and knowledge of fire door standards including BS 9999 and BS 8214. Renova Projects UK's inspectors are trained and experienced in fire door compliance, and we provide a written report with findings and recommendations after every inspection.
What does a fire door inspection involve? ▼
A fire door inspection covers all critical components: the door leaf and frame condition, gap tolerances (maximum 3mm between door and frame, 8mm at the floor), intumescent strips and smoke seals, self-closing devices, hinges and ironmongery, glazing and vision panels, and certification labels and signage. We issue a written compliance report with photographs on completion.
What legislation requires fire door inspections? ▼
Fire door maintenance and inspection is required under the Regulatory Reform (Fire Safety) Order 2005 for commercial premises, and under the Fire Safety Act 2021 and Fire Safety (England) Regulations 2022 for residential buildings with two or more storeys. The Responsible Person is legally required to ensure fire doors are maintained in an effective condition.
Do you carry out fire door remedial works? ▼
Yes. Where our fire door inspection identifies defects, we can carry out all necessary remedial works — from replacing intumescent strips and seals to fitting new self-closing devices, replacing ironmongery or supplying and fitting new certified fire doors. We provide a fixed-price quote for all remedial works following the inspection report.
