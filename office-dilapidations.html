What Are Dilapidations?

Understanding your
lease-end obligations

Office dilapidations are the repairs, removals, and reinstatement works a tenant must complete to return a leased commercial space to its agreed condition when the lease ends. These obligations are defined in your lease and documented in a schedule of dilapidations prepared by a chartered surveyor.

Whether you're a tenant planning your exit or a landlord preparing for re-letting, our team manages every obligation — clearly, efficiently, and within budget.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does an office dilapidations project take? ▼
Timelines depend on the scale of works required and the terms of your lease. Simple dilapidation works might be completed in a few days or weeks, while larger reinstatement projects can take longer. We always provide a clear programme so you know exactly what to expect.
What's included in office dilapidation works? ▼
Dilapidation works typically include strip-out of tenant installations, structural and cosmetic repairs, reinstatement of partitions and finishes, redecoration, and any other works specified in your schedule of dilapidations and lease agreement.
Who is responsible for paying for dilapidation works? ▼
Responsibility is defined by your lease. Generally tenants are responsible for returning the space to the condition required under the lease terms. The extent of liability can often be negotiated — our transparent process helps establish a fair, agreed scope from the outset.
Do you work with chartered surveyors? ▼
Yes — we collaborate closely with chartered surveyors throughout the process. We can work from an existing schedule of dilapidations or assist in reviewing and scoping works prior to a formal schedule being issued.
Which areas do you cover for dilapidations work? ▼
Our primary coverage area is London, Kent, Surrey, Essex and the wider South East. We also take on projects nationally for established clients. Get in touch to discuss your specific location.
